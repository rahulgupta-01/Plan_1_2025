## Task overview

For this assessment task, you will complete 2 parts by designing a 1000-word lesson plan and an 8-minute video presentation Uploaded to YouTube as UNLISTED and the link placed at the top of the lesson plan or on the cover page in the Assignment to justify your teaching decisions. Your lesson plan will be designed for a specific year level and learning area, incorporating explicit instruction and inclusive teaching strategies that support diverse learners.

### Part A:

You will create a detailed 1-hour lesson plan using the SCU Lesson Plan Template (available in Blackboard under Assessment 2).

Refer to Workshop 6 to engage with the video and activity. This is an important foundation in planning your learning experience. As you plan, consider cognitive load, memory and novice/expert learners.

To develop your lesson plan:

• Select a year level and learning area (e.g., primary literacy, secondary mathematics).

• Use the SCU Lesson Plan Template to structure your plan effectively.

• Ensure curriculum alignment by incorporating learning outcomes from the NSW syllabus or Australian Curriculum.

• Outline very clearly, your use of explicit teaching, modelling and scaffolding strategies, clear learning intentions and success criteria.

• Include a brief description of behaviour management strategies you will employ in this lesson.

• Embed the GC’s literacy, numeracy and the use of ICT in your plan.

• Identify two diverse learners from different categories (e.g., an EAL/D student and a student with autism) and include differentiation strategies to support their learning

• Plan formative assessment techniques that align with the lesson objectives and provide meaningful feedback.

### Part B:

In your 8-minute video presentation, Uploaded to YouTube as UNLISTED and the link placed at the top of the lesson plan or on the cover page in the Assignment, you will discuss how you included explicit teaching etc. (see below) in the lesson plan. Importantly, you must justify the teaching, differentiation, and assessment strategies in your lesson plan. Your explanation should focus on why these strategies are effective for the content, year level, and diverse learners. Your presentation must include visuals of your lesson plan and be supported by relevant images and academic references.

The following topics must also be discussed in the video presentation:

1. Why explicit teaching is a quality instruction in reducing the need for further intervention (how your knowledge of learner development and schema development prevents cognitive load). Can you identify any neuromyths in the plan? How can these be avoided?

2. How ICT was used ethically in this lesson.

3. Explain how the classroom was organised for this lesson and how this enabled student participation, well-being and safety.

4. How community/parents/carers could be involved in this lesson.

5. Identify an area of professional development that you could pursue following your reflection on this lesson.

### Part A: Lesson Plan (1000 Words)

Create a detailed 1-hour lesson plan using the provided Lesson Plan Template (Located in your Blackboard site/Assessment 2 folder). To begin your lesson plan:

• Select a year level and learning area (e.g., primary literacy, secondary mathematics).

• Use the SCU Lesson Plan Template located to structure your lesson.

• Include key teaching strategies, instructional sequences, and behaviour management strategies.

### Part B: Video Presentation (8 mins) Uploaded to YouTube as UNLISTED and the link placed at the top of the lesson plan or on the cover page in the Assignment.

In your 8-minute video presentation, you will justify the teaching, differentiation, and assessment strategies used in your lesson plan. Your explanation should focus on why these strategies are effective for the content, year level, and diverse learners in your class.

As part of your presentation, you must identify two diverse learners from different categories and explain how your lesson is designed to support them. Choose two students from the list in the task description.

Your presentation should focus on justification rather than description. Assume your marker (and future mentor teacher) already understands the basic elements of a lesson plan. Instead of describing what is included, explain why your choices are effective in meeting student needs, curriculum requirements, and learning objectives.

Reminders:

• Stick to the 8-minute time limit – Your marker will stop watching at 8.30, so ensure your presentation is concise and well-paced.

• Keep slides visually engaging – Avoid large blocks of text. Instead, use keywords, images, and excerpts from your lesson plan to reinforce your key points.

• Prioritise clear narration – What you say is the most important part of your presentation. Your slides should act as visual aids, not a script.

• Orally cite references during your presentation – When discussing research, mention the author’s name naturally in your speech (e.g., “According to Smith (2020), differentiated instruction…”). Ensure all sources are correctly cited in your APA 7th reference list on the final slide.

• Ensure you save and include your transcript and reference list for submission with your lesson plan. You may append the transcript and reference list to the end of your lesson plan document.

• Use PowerPoint or similar software – If you're unsure how to record your presentation, watch this tutorial: How to Record a PowerPoint Presentation.

• Avoid "Death by PowerPoint" – Too much text can overwhelm your audience. Use visuals and animations strategically to enhance engagement. For tips, watch this TED Talk: How to Avoid Death by PowerPoint.

• Ensure your video is accessible before submission – Your YouTube video must be set to "Unlisted" (not Private). Test the link by opening it in an incognito browser or sharing it with a friend. Further details for YouTube submission are listed in the Task Submission area of this brief.

## Suggested Presentation Structure

### 1. Introduction (Approx. 1 minute)

• Title slide – Include your name, unit details, and the topic of your lesson.

• Lesson background:

o Identify the selected outcome (NSW syllabus) / content descriptor (Australian Curriculum) that your lesson addresses. Be realistic—one or two outcomes per lesson is sufficient.

o State the AITSL teaching standard you are focusing on and explain how it applies to your lesson.

o Clearly define the Learning Intention (LI) and Success Criteria (SC) in student-friendly language. Read this resource from AISTL for guidance.

o Briefly describe prior learning—what knowledge or skills students already have to support their success in this lesson.

o Identify your two diverse learners and provide relevant background information that explains their learning needs.

### 2. Justification of Teaching Strategies (Approx. 1.5 minutes)

• Explain the teaching strategies and resources included in your lesson.

• Justify how these strategies create a safe, engaging, and supportive learning environment.

• Link your explanation to relevant educational theories and research.

### 3. Discussion (Approx. 1.5 minutes)

• on the use of explicit teaching with links to learner development,

• the use of ICT,

• the inclusion of parent/community involvement in this lesson,

• and an area for future professional development.

### 4. Justification of Differentiation Strategies (Approx. 1.5 minutes)

• Explain how your differentiation strategies for your lesson is adapted for the two diverse learners you selected.

• Justify why these differentiation strategies are effective in supporting student engagement and success.

• Link your differentiation approach to inclusive education principles and relevant academic sources.

### 5. Justification of Formative Assessment (Approx. 2 minutes)

• Explain the Assessment for Learning (AfL) / formative assessment strategies in your lesson.

• Justify how these assessments help measure student progress and provide effective feedback.

• Link your assessment strategies to Module 4 (Assessment & Feedback) and relevant research.

### 6. Conclusion (Approx. 30 seconds)

• Summarise the key points in a clear and professional manner.

• Reflect on how your lesson plan aligns with best teaching practices and supports student learning.

### 7. Reference List (Final Slide, Not Included in Time Limit)

• Display a properly formatted APA 7th reference list on your last slide.

• The time spent displaying the reference list does not count towards the 8-minute limit.

### How do I include in-text references if I’m speaking?

It is very important to include references to your academic sources when giving an academic presentation. When writing, you would include the in-text reference at the beginning or end of a sentence. For example, “According to Smith (2019), literacy is important because…” or “…the importance of being literate (Smith, 2019)”. In an oral presentation, you refer to the author’s name in a sentence while you are speaking. You can omit the year as the viewer can access this information later on in your reference list. For example, “Smith explains that literacy is important…” or “This idea is supported by Smith…”. Ensure it sounds natural in the sentence when you speak. To emphasise the verbal reference, you can also include it in written form on the slide you are talking about (i.e., author’s name and year of publication in APA 7th), this is very helpful for the viewer / your marker.
