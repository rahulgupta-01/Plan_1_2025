## What You Will Do:

In this task, you will design a poster to demonstrate your Philosophy of Education, with a three minute voice-over explaining how your understanding of teaching and learning is informed by key theories, frameworks, standards, codes of ethics, principles, and priorities. Your philosophy must clearly demonstrate your understanding of core concepts (CCDs), supported by clear explanations and examples focussing on the Australian Professional Standards for Teachers (APST). Finally, you will provide a written rationale for how your philosophy will guide your future professional learning and support improved student learning outcomes.

For this assessment, you will create three components:

- 1-page graphic poster  
- a 3-minute voice-over explaining your poster. How to submit your video with narration using PowerPoint

**N.B.** Your Assessment 1 should be submitted as a word or PDF document with a link to your Unlisted YouTube recording of the 3-minute voice over on the cover page and Ppt slides. Please use the template provided below in Resources. Turnitin does not recognise PowerPoint documents for submission.

The poster will visually represent your philosophy of education through visuals, diagrams, and minimal text. The rationale, presented in 4 slides (or one page with 4 paragraphs) on a PowerPoint presentation, will explain the poster in detail and include references to educational theories, frameworks, standards, principles, and priorities covered in Modules 1 and 2. You are required to provide a voice-over, explaining your poster and using your rationale to demonstrate and substantiate your understanding of the framework choices you made. The relevant APST’s must also be included in the rationale. This task allows you to demonstrate critical analysis by evaluating and integrating relevant theories into your teaching philosophy, reflection by connecting personal experiences and learning styles to professional practice, and professional communication by articulating and visually presenting complex ideas with clarity and creativity. The completed assessment will consist of a professionally designed poster in PDF format, with a voice-over explanation of the poster.

The second page of the document will consist of a detailed, APA-referenced explanation and rationale.

Visual communication is an essential strategy in teaching. The poster enables you to visually communicate your philosophy of education. The voice-over enables you to practice your verbal presentation skills and ensure that your message is clearly articulated and concisely presented. Play the voice-over for a classmate and receive peer feedback before you submit!

## Task Instructions

Follow these steps to complete the assessment:

### Part 1: Create Your Poster (1 Page)

- Design a 1-page poster (Powerpoint, Presi,Canva) that visually represents your philosophy of education.  
- Include visuals (e.g., diagrams, infographics, mind maps) and minimal text (this text will not count towards the word count).  
- Highlight key beliefs about teaching and learning, grounded in educational theories and frameworks.  
- Use tools: Canva, PowerPoint, or Prezi.  
- Ensure visuals and text are relevant, clear, professional, and free of plagiarism.  

### Part 2: Create your voice-over.

- Prepare your 3-minute voice-over (markers will stop listening at 3:20).  
- You will explain what you have included in your poster, and most importantly, why. Substantiate your decisions, using your module information to support your discussion.  
- Embed the link at the end of your poster page.  

### Part 3: Write Your Rationale and Supporting Discussion (4 PowerPoint slides or one page/4 paragraphs)

- Paragraph 1: Write a brief introduction including your name, course, specialisations and reasons for choosing the teaching profession. Provide an overview of your philosophy, including its core principles and priorities.  
- Paragraph 2: Explanation of how theories, frameworks, standards, and principles from Modules 1 and 2 have shaped your philosophy.  
- Paragraph 3: discuss how intercultural development, responsiveness to diversity, and self-reflection are positioned in the framework you have chosen.  
- Paragraph 4: Reflect on your personal experiences of learning. As a learner, provide a concrete example of how you moved from novice to expert, using primary and secondary knowledge and schema development to learn and understand a skill or concept.  
- Your rationale must explicitly reference the Australian Professional Standards for Teachers (APSTs) that align with your educational philosophy.  
- Use professional language and APA 7th referencing.  

- Ensure you also review the following requirements:  
- Is your poster visually engaging, with minimal text (short sentence or phrase for each visual)?  
- Have you clearly linked your philosophy to educational theories and frameworks?  
- Does your rationale demonstrate critical engagement and reflection?  
- Have you included a complete reference list in APA 7th format?  
- Is your submission formatted correctly using the assessment template?
