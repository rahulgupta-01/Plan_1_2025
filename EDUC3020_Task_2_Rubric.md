# Task 2 Marking Rubric

## **1. Design of learning experiences (engagement, creativity/innovation, literacy & numeracy) 20%**

**HD+**  
Unit sequence (6 weeks × 4 lessons) is exceptionally coherent and demonstrates innovative design beyond standard expectations. At least 4 varied, high-impact strategies per week that actively engage students (e.g., inquiry, collaboration, design thinking).

Creativity/innovation is deeply embedded with measurable success criteria. Literacy and numeracy integration is sophisticated and discipline-specific, explicitly stated in learning intentions and activities.

Student independence is strongly promoted through structured choice, reflection, and self-assessment tools.

**HD**  
Unit sequence is complete and coherent. At least 3 varied strategies per week that actively engage students.

Creativity/innovation is explicitly targeted with success criteria. Literacy and numeracy are clearly integrated with relevant activities and learning intentions.

Student independence is supported through choice and reflection opportunities.

**D**  
Sequence is complete with minor gaps. At least 3 strategies per week that engage students;

creativity/innovation evident in most weeks. Literacy and numeracy are integrated with mostly explicit links.

Some opportunities for student independence (e.g., occasional choice or reflection).

**C**  
Sequence is present but uneven. At least 2 engaging strategies per week;

creativity/innovation present in some weeks. Literacy or numeracy integrated with links sometimes implicit.

Limited independence-building opportunities.

**P**  
Sequence is present but uneven with gaps. At least 1 engaging strategy per week.

Creativity/innovation noted but not systematically designed. Literacy or numeracy evident but not explicitly planned.

Minimal independence-building.

**MF**  
Sequence has several gaps. Engagement strategies intermittent.

Creativity/innovation noted but not designed or inappropriate for content. Literacy or numeracy evident but weakly connected.

Minimal independence-building.

**F**  
Unit sequence missing or fragmented. Activities are primarily teacher-directed with little engagement. No meaningful literacy/numeracy integration.

**N/A**  
No attempt or evidence of unauthorised AI use.

**MF**

Sequencing weak or inappropriate. Significant misunderstanding of syllabus requirements and assessment policy.

**F**  
Outcomes missing or incorrect. No meaningful mapping or sequencing. Major misunderstanding of syllabus/curriculum.

**N/A**

UDL principles are deeply embedded (multiple means of engagement, representation, expression) across lessons and assessments.

Adjustments are highly tailored and demonstrably eliminate barriers, ensuring equity beyond compliance.

Assessment adjustments clearly articulated and eliminate barriers to equity.

**D**  
Informed, specific, and reasonable adjustments documented for all identified groups. UDL principles applied appropriately.

Assessment adjustments noted and suitable for equity.

**C**  
Specific, reasonable adjustments documented for all identified groups. UDL principles considered and applied in some areas.

Assessment adjustments evident but lack detail or consistency.

**P**

Assessment adjustments present but generic or insufficiently tailored.

**MF**  
Adjustments mentioned but inadequate, vague, ineffective, or for only one group. UDL principles weakly applied.

Assessment adjustments minimal or inappropriate.

**F**  
Very limited adjustments mentioned, vague, or for only one group. No evidence of UDL principles.

No meaningful teaching or assessment adjustments.

**N/A**  
No evidence of adjustments or consideration for diversity.
